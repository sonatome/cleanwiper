DIY Pest Prevention Tips

Published: October 1, 2024

Keeping pests out of your home is often easier than getting them out. By following a few simple and consistent maintenance steps, you can significantly reduce the risk of infestations year-round without always resorting to harsh chemicals.

1. Seal All Entry Points

Pests can squeeze through the smallest cracks. Take a walk around your home's exterior and inspect for openings. Use **caulk** to seal gaps around utility lines, pipes, and window frames. Pay special attention to weep holes and foundation cracks.

2. Manage Moisture and Water

Most pests, especially termites and mosquitoes, thrive in damp environments. Fix leaky faucets, pipes, and air conditioning units immediately. Ensure your gutters are clean and draining water away from the foundation of your house. Ventilate crawl spaces and attics to reduce humidity.

3. Proper Food Storage

Ants, roaches, and rodents are constantly searching for food. Store all food items, including pet food, in sealed, airtight containers. Clean up spills immediately and avoid leaving dirty dishes in the sink overnight. Regularly empty and clean indoor garbage bins.

4. Declutter Your Yard

Firewood piles, overgrown shrubs, and construction debris provide perfect hiding places for pests. Keep shrubbery trimmed away from the house walls. Store firewood at least 20 feet away from the structure and off the ground. By minimizing outdoor harborage, you reduce the chances of pests making their way inside.
